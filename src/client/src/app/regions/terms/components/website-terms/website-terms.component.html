Welcome to DertInfo.

DertInfo is a website that supplies services to you and “Dancing England Rapper Tournament” (DERT) organisers to support the running of the annual event.

We act as an intermediary between event organisers and groups to support the communication of your desired engagement at the organisers event.

We support the publication of results in order that teams and team members can view results once published by the organisers and also publish openly to the public the amalgamated results.

Our DataPolicy explains how we collect and use data you provide to us.

The services that we provide.

Our objective is to make the running of the annual Dancing England Rapper Tournament as simple as possible for those involved. To do this we provide the following services described below:

Allow group administrators to store and manage group team and member information

Each year a group needs to inform the organisers of a DERT as to their desire to attend the event. This involves letting the organiser know how many teams they wish to enter, which competitions they wish to enter and how many tickets and of what types they will need for their members.

We store this information for group administrators in order that they can select the teams and members from the stored information to create a new registration for the event.

A group's previous results and comments are retained and made available to groups to allow teams to review and choose to act up to feedback from earlier competitions. This information also supports the classification of team entries into subsequent years (premier, champtionship, open).

Allow group members to view competition outputs

Group members may access the system and gain visibility of detailed scores and comments and attendance / entry information.

Access for a user to see a group's information is provided to the user by the group administrator providing that user with a “token/password” to use to authorise the user to view the group information. The user can then use this token to be identified as a member of the group.

Allow visibility of competition outcomes and group information to non users.

Dert info is intended to be a central resource for being able to view results of the DERT competition. In order that we fulfil this objective an event organiser will publish the results of the competitions. In doing so this will list on the website the group name and possibly the groups chosen image on the public part of the site. Along with the abbreviated scores that the teams achieved.

As a group administrator you accept that the primary image and your team name can be shared for this purpose even where the group visibility is set to private.

Allow construction of an event

The organising committee of a DERT creates a new event on the DertInfo system. The creation of this event allows the organisers to specify what tickets are available for sale (activities) and what the prices will be for those activities.

Activities are available for either teams or individuals. For example a particular competition is an example of a team activity and indoor camping may be an example of an individual activity.

The organiser will determine what their DERT will involve and the prices for it’s activities. Once constructed they will open the event for registration. This will make the event visible to any groups using the service and allow groups to register for the event.

All information about the event is visible to the groups including the intended prices for the activities.

Connect groups to events and event activities.

Groups can express their interest in a DERT by submitting a registration. They can submit this information to the event when the event organiser opens their event for registration.

The registration for the event in the first instance does not need to be complete but allows the event to gauge a level of interest.

The activity of creating a registration by the group is an expression of interest by the group to attend the event. By submitting the registration the group provides their contact information with the event and any information contained within the registration.

It is common that details of a registration change as the event gets closer. DertInfo supports both the teams and the organisers by allowing amendments to registrations up until a close date at which point the registration are locked.

Past to point that the registrations are locked then only the organiser can make amendments to a registration.

During the registration process groups will be able to see the considered costs for their registration and this will form the basis of the cost to the group for entry when the registration close date is reached.

At the close of registration the event organisers will ask all groups to pay the indicated amount on their registrations. This is done by an email being sent by the system with the outline of the costs on this email.

Support consistency of the DERT competition format

Competitions are a type of team activity which teams within a group will request entry to at registration.

A DERT typically has 4 competitions. These are: The Main Competition, Spotlight, Traditional and veterans.

An individual competition is run at one or more venues and has a number of judges per venue.

DertInfo allows the event organiser to construct the competitions based on templates from running the previous DERT. These templates support the specifications of marking criteria categories and their weightings. The number of venues (translating to the number of dances per team) and the judges per dance.

Based on the number of judges score sets can be created so that the judges in a venue may be judging different marking criteria.

Each team entered into a competition may be eligible for an award. Eligibility for an award is decided by the organiser and assigned to the team. Examples of awards are: Premier, Championship, Best Newcomer.

Based on the number of teams that enter via the registration. DertInfo builds a profile of the competitions in order that all expected dances are prepared and progress of the competition can be tracked by the organisers.

When all expected dances are completed then the organizer can publish the results of the competition.

After result publication the results will be made available in the website which will display the collated scores for each award. Additionally groups and their members will be able to view in their account the details of each dance along with any score sheets and comments from the judges.

Provide other technology solutions to support DERT

Other services may be provided where a normal DERT as outlined cannot be run and these additional services may make use of stored information to accommodate the running of these associated events.
