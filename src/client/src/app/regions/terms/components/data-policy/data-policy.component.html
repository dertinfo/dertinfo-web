Data Policy

We believe that you should know what data we collect from you, why we ask for it and how we use it.

We also believe that you should have control over what information you provide and how we use it.

We outline this in this privacy policy in order to give you the best information to inform your decisions.

1. Information you supply:

Basic Account Information

In order to have an account with DertInfo you provide us your name and email address.

DertInfo uses a third party supplier for storage of all authentication information in order to maximize the security of your personal data. All signup and login is managed through the authentication providers API using a web token mechanism.

Events and Activity information

An “event” is an instance of the annual Dancing England Rapper Tournament (DERT).

In order to run the event then we collect contact information, the name and promotional image of the event along with what dates the event is on, where the event is and when you would like to publicise the event through our services.

We allow you to select what the visibility of your event is in order to give you control over if we publish the event publicly on the website, we only show the event to registered users or we only show it to specifically invited users.

In most cases the Dancing England Rapper Tournament will be a public event. However the ability to remove the listing may be required and to facilitate that removal the event can be changed to private.

In order to accommodate consistency from each DERT to the next we store templates for the Dancing England Rapper Tournament. This template is the standard setup of competitions and score categories that are used. DertInfo supplies these templates to you in order to simplify the configuration of your event. You are not required to use the template and can construct your event as you choose.

To configure your event you provide us information about “activities” these are the items that either group members and teams can participate in. Typically you will inform us of the price that you wish to change for those activities and when you publicise the event those activities and prices will be visible to the target audience based on the visibility setting of the event.

After creation and publication of the event, based on your visibility settings groups will be able to see your event and express their interest through the registration process to attend the event.

When a group registers for an event they will receive an email from you using the email template that you define through our email provider disclosing your email address to the registering team. In order that follow up communication between the group and the event can be conducted.

At time of registration attendance is not guaranteed and only when a registration is confirmed which typically happens at the end of the registration period is the group notified.

During the registration period as defined by the dates supplied both the event organiser and the team organiser can change the registration and acts a communication bridge as to what activities the teams and members within the group participate in. See Registration for more details.

Groups, teams and members information

A “group” is a collection of teams and members. Where the teams and members participate in activities published by an event.

A group information is made visible to others based on the visibility settings of the group. These visibility settings are Public Restricted or Private.

Public - The group name it’s biography and image can be displayed in the public part of the site and visible to non users

Restricted - The group information is not displayed publicly but may be visible to other users of the services

Private - The group cannot be discovered on the system by any means and only through direct engagement with an event is their presence on the system known.

Note that the visibility setting of a group name and images will not prevent the group name being displayed in the result listing where there is engagement of that group to enter a competition and the amalgamation of the results for that competition are displayed publicly.

Information about Group members is always private until such a point that a registration is submitted see the registration for further details.

Competitions

A “competition” is a special type of activity that has a number of attributes such as marking criteria.

As an entrant into a competition we record that you have entered into the competition and when the results for the competition are published we produce an amalgamated scores to produce a league table and we publish that league table publicly on the website. In doing this the name of the “team” is published. As each group may have more than one team that entered the competition.

Additionally we store the detailed score information along with the comments given to you by the competition judges and make that available to the group and group members of the entrant on the publication of the results.

In order to produce the paperwork judging of the competitions then we are provided the judges names in order that the score sheets can be headed with their names when produced. This allows the judge to get the correct score set for their speciality of knowledge.

In some cases a competition also has “venues”. The details of those venues will be entered into the system and those venues are displayed on the top of the score sheets.

Score sheets (and therefore the venue, the name of the judge and their comments) are made available to the group and group members when the results are published by the event organiser.

By a judge participating in the event it is the responsibility of the event organiser to make this clear to the judges of the competition when they agree to participate in the event as the judges have no direct engagement with these services.

Additionally the venue should be made aware by the event organiser that their venue may be advertised through our provided services as communication to where the teams will be competing and to audiences that may wish to watch.

Registrations

“Registrations” are the mechanism with which information is shared between group administrators and event organisers.

Registrations contain group, team and member information along with activity attendance.

Group Information includes: The group name image and bio. This is so that the event can use this information in the construction of marketing material or programme.

Team Information includes: The team name and the team image for use as per the group information and the activities that the team wishes to participate in. This information is used to enter the team into the correct competitions and supports the construction of the paperwork and scoring framework for the competition.

Group Member Information includes: The group members name and dob (where supplied and required) in order that wrist bands (of the correct type, concessions etc) may be allocated for the specific person. The activities that each member wishes to participate in so that the correct tickets can be allocated. This may be such that the ticket allocated allows access to certain venues and therefore is required to be identifiable to the individual. This may be for the purposes of insurance of the venue by the organiser or any other such purpose where the identity of the individual would be required. To ensure correct allocation of tickets.

2. Information we receive

User Account

Through your engagement with our services we keep track of which groups and events you have access to and to how you are permitted to engage with those groups or events.

Groups Events Registration

From the interaction with the system between the event organisers and the group administrators we gain insights into attendance trends that can inform future events as to the facilities to supply..

From this information there are a number of things that we can deduce from the data about team performance at the competitions over time.

The collation of information is primarily for the benefit of the event in order to determine capacities and costs and revenues.

4. Managing your information:

Keeping your information up to date:

In most circumstances you have the ability to update and maintain your user account information and preferences through the user information section of the website. This includes updating your name and telephone number.

Also in this user section you should be able to see some of the information relating to your account that we have identified based on your interactions with the service. Including but not limited to when you agreed to our terms and conditions.

We provide this information in order to be as transparent as possible as to the data that we hold about you.

Deletion

Also in the user account section you have the ability to delete your account. On deleting your account any information that you have provided to us will also be deleted or obfuscated depending on whether that data is attached to information that is required for the system to function.

Be aware that other third parties may have received information from this site during the period that your information was available.

5. Non Personal Information

We share non personal information to event organisers where this information would assist in the facilitation or improvement of running the DERT. This information includes but is not limited to data such as attendance volume in the previous year or previous years rankings in order that teams can be allocated for the correct awards.

We may also share information such as engagement with a feature that we’ve provided.

7. Special

DERT Of DERTs

In the case of some features not using a full login (specifically DertOfDerts) you provide us with your email address and name. We store this information encrypted in our database in order to protect your identity.

By participating in DERT of DERTs you consent that the scores that you submit and the comments that you enter will be forwarded to participating teams anonymously. It is your responsibility to ensure that your comments in this special event do not contain any information that would permit the transmission of any data that you would not want to be seen by the participating teams or their members.

For DertOfDerts we collect your name and email information so that we can contact you if you would be interested in being a judge at a future Dancing England Rapper Tournament.

By participating in DERT of DERTs and if you choose that you would like to be contacted with regard to being a judge at a future event then you consent that we may share that information with a DERT organiser where appropriate. If you want to revoke this interest please email us.

DERT of DERTs stores information in the browser. Your name is stored in the session storage and will be cleared when the browser is closed. You will be able to recover your session if you provide the correct matching email address but this is limited to and rendered useless after 6 days no longer permitting recovery of your session.
